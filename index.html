Financial Literacy

Set Your Self up For A Better Financial Future

Over 77% of Americans are anxious about their financial situation. With the current financial state of America it is good to not only make good money, but also know how to save and invest it. In CCLs financial Literacy Course we teach:

Money Management

The Bureau of Labor Statistics(BLS) reports the median annual wage for computer and technology occupations was $97,430 in 2021.
This is incredible right, but just because you make good money does not mean you can not learn how to manage it.
Here at CCL we teach you just that. You learn the following:

  1. Creating a budget that outlines income and expenses
  2. Tracking spending to identify areas for improvement
  3. Prioritizing expenses to ensure bills and necessary payments are made on time
  4. Setting financial goals, such as saving for a down payment on a house or paying off debt
  5. Saving money regularly, even if it's a small amount each month
  6. Minimizing unnecessary expenses, such as eating out or subscriptions
  7. Investing money in a diversified portfolio to grow wealth over time

  9. Building an emergency fund to cover unexpected expenses
